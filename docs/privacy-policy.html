2. Overview of Information Categories and the On-Device vs Cloud Distinction

Before describing each category of information in detail in the sections that follow, this section provides a map of the types of information the App involves and explains the fundamental distinction between on-device and cloud processing that shapes how that information is handled.

Categories of information. Information associated with your use of the App falls into five broad categories:

  • Account and identity data, such as the email address, display name, and account identifier associated with your Firebase Authentication account, which you provide directly or which is passed to us by Google when you sign in with Google Sign-In (see Section 3);
  • Usage, performance, and diagnostic data, collected through Firebase Analytics and Firebase Crashlytics, including event logs, session information, feature-usage signals, and crash reports, together with the device identifiers and tokens used by Firebase Cloud Messaging to deliver optional push notifications (see Section 4);
  • Advertising data, including identifiers and signals processed by Google AdMob in connection with the advertisements served to users on the Free plan, including rewarded advertisements shown when you reach the export limit (see Section 5);
  • Subscription and purchase data, including entitlement status, product identifiers, and transaction events processed through Google Play Billing and RevenueCat in connection with the Free, Pro Monthly, Pro Yearly, and Pro Lifetime plans (see Section 6); and
  • Device-local preferences, such as your selected voice preset, your running export counter, and the flag indicating whether you have completed onboarding, stored exclusively on your device using Android SharedPreferences and never transmitted to us (see Section 7).

Your audio and video content, including recordings you make within the App and media files you import for processing, is in a category apart: it is your content, processed on your behalf, and under the on-device path it does not become our data at all.

The on-device path. The default audio and video processing pipeline of the App runs entirely within the application on your Android device. When you record audio through the microphone, import an audio or video file from device storage, or process media through the on-device noise-cancellation, voice-enhancement, or vocal and music separation functions, that media is never uploaded to any server. The machine-learning models, spectral algorithms, and signal-processing pipelines that perform the denoising and enhancement computation are bundled with the App or stored locally on your device; they do not require a network connection and do not transmit your audio or video anywhere. We therefore do not receive, store, process, or have access to the content of media that you process through the default on-device path. The statement "your audio never leaves your phone" accurately describes this path.

The optional cloud path. The App makes available one optional cloud-based processing engine: integration with the ElevenLabs Audio Isolation API. This engine is never selected automatically. It is available only if you expressly navigate to the relevant setting within the App, select the ElevenLabs engine, and supply your own ElevenLabs API key. If you take all three of those steps and then submit an audio recording for processing through that engine, the audio you choose to process is transmitted over HTTPS to ElevenLabs' servers and processed under ElevenLabs' terms of service and privacy policy, which you should review before enabling this feature. We do not receive a copy of that audio and do not log its contents; what the App transmits goes directly to ElevenLabs using your own credentials. We receive back the processed output for display within the App. Because this path involves deliberate, user-initiated transmission using the user's own credentials, we describe it throughout this Policy wherever its data-flow differs from the on-device default.

Why this distinction matters. Many data-protection obligations — including the rights described later in this Policy to access, correct, or delete personal data — apply to data that we hold or control. Because we do not hold or control the content of on-device-processed media, those rights, as between you and us, do not apply to that content. They do apply to the account, usage, advertising, and billing data described in the sections that follow, and we address each category's rights implications in those sections. Understanding whether a given piece of information was processed on-device or through a cloud service is therefore important context for understanding what choices and rights you have.

Minimal-collection principle. The App is designed to collect only the information necessary for authentication, operational integrity, advertising, and subscription management. We do not build behavioural profiles, sell your data, or use your information for purposes unrelated to providing and improving the App. The sections that follow describe in concrete terms what information each category involves, why it is collected, how long it is retained, who can see it, and what rights you have in respect of it.

3. Information You Provide: Account and Identity Data

This section describes the categories of Personal Data that you actively provide to us, or that are generated about you, when you create or use an Account and otherwise identify yourself to the App. The information addressed here is distinct from the usage, diagnostic, advertising, and billing information described in later sections; here we are concerned with the data through which you establish and maintain an authenticated identity. Because the App is designed so that most processing occurs on your device, the quantity of identity data we handle is deliberately limited, and much of it is processed on our behalf by Firebase Authentication rather than collected directly by us.

Authentication and the data you supply. The App uses Firebase Authentication, a service operated by Google, to create and manage your Account. You may authenticate using either of two methods, and the data you provide differs accordingly:

  • Google Sign-In. If you choose to sign in with your Google account, Firebase Authentication receives from Google a set of profile attributes associated with that account. This typically includes the email address linked to your Google account, your display name, a stable account identifier, and, where available, the URL of your profile photograph. We do not receive your Google password and never have access to it; authentication is handled entirely by Google's systems, which return to the App a token confirming your identity.
  • Email and password. If you choose to register or sign in using an email address and a password, you supply your email address and a password of your choosing. The password is transmitted to and managed by Firebase Authentication, which stores it in a hashed and salted form under Google's security controls. We do not store, and are not able to view, your plaintext password. We may, through Firebase, send you verification, password-reset, and similar service messages at the email address you provide.

In both cases, the email address you use becomes the primary identifier associated with your Account, and you are responsible for ensuring that it is accurate and that you are entitled to use it.

Account identifiers and authentication state. Upon successful authentication, Firebase assigns your Account a unique user identifier (commonly referred to as a Firebase UID). This identifier is a non-public, system-generated string that allows the App to recognize you across sessions and devices without repeatedly transmitting your credentials. We use the Firebase UID, together with the issued authentication tokens, to maintain your signed-in state, to associate your preferences and entitlements with you, and to link your Account to your Subscription status as managed through RevenueCat. The authentication tokens that keep you signed in are held securely on your device and are refreshed by the Firebase software development kit; they are not credentials that you need to manage manually, and they can be invalidated by signing out.

Information generated when you use your Account. Beyond the data you directly enter, a limited amount of identity-adjacent information is generated in the course of operating your Account. This may include the method by which you signed in (Google or email and password), timestamps indicating when your Account was created and when you last authenticated, the verification status of your email address, and the linkage between your Firebase UID and the corresponding customer record maintained by RevenueCat for the purpose of recognizing your plan (Free, Pro Monthly, Pro Yearly, or Pro Lifetime). Where you contact us directly, the contents of your correspondence and the email address from which you write also constitute information you provide, and we use it to respond to and document your request.

Why we process this information. We process your Account and identity data for purposes that are necessary to provide the App and that are limited to what those purposes require. Specifically, we use this information to:

  • create, authenticate, and secure your Account, and to recognize you when you return to the App;
  • associate your purchases and entitlements with you so that your Pro features are correctly delivered across sessions and, where applicable, across devices;
  • communicate with you regarding your Account, including verification, security, and password-reset messages and responses to your inquiries;
  • detect, investigate, and prevent fraudulent, unauthorized, or abusive use of Accounts; and
  • comply with our legal obligations and to establish, exercise, or defend legal claims where necessary.

The legal bases on which we rely for this processing, under Applicable Data Protection Law, include the performance of our agreement with you (because an Account is required to access certain features), your consent where you voluntarily provide information, our legitimate interests in securing the App and preventing abuse, and compliance with applicable legal obligations. Under India's DPDP Act, our processing of your Account data generally rests on the consent you give when you create an Account, or on legitimate uses recognized by that statute, and you may withdraw consent as described in the rights section of this Policy.

Your control over identity data. Providing Account and identity data is, for the most part, a matter of your choice, although certain features cannot function without an authenticated identity. You may review and update the email address and display name associated with your Account through the sign-in provider you selected, and you may change your password through the standard reset mechanism. You may sign out at any time, which clears the authentication tokens held on your device. If you wish to delete your Account and the associated identity data, you may request deletion by contacting us at noiseclear.review@gmail.com; upon such a request we will delete or de-identify the Account record we control and the associated Firebase Authentication entry, subject to any information we are required to retain for legal, accounting, security, or fraud-prevention purposes, and subject to the independent retention practices of the Third-Party Services that process this data on our behalf.

4. Audio, Video, and Other Content You Submit

This section explains how the App handles the recordings, media files, and related material that you create, import, or process using it. We refer to this material collectively as "Content." Content includes the audio you capture through your microphone, the video you capture through your camera, the audio and video files you import from your device storage, the separated stems, enhanced tracks, and isolated voice or music outputs that the App produces, and any temporary or intermediate data generated while a file is being processed. Because the App is a tool for noise cancellation, voice enhancement, and vocal and music separation, Content is the most sensitive category of information you may handle through the App, and we treat the distinction between on-device and cloud processing as central to how we describe our practices.

On-device processing is the default, and your audio stays with you. By default, the App performs noise cancellation, voice enhancement, and vocal and music separation locally on your device using on-device models. On this default path, your Content is read, processed, and written entirely on your phone. We do not upload it, we do not receive it, we do not store it on our servers, and we have no technical ability to access, listen to, or view it. Consistent with how we describe the App, on the on-device path your audio never leaves your phone. The same applies to video that you process on-device and to the output files that the App generates from your Content. This means that, for the overwhelming majority of ordinary use, the substance of what you record and process is known only to you and remains under your sole control on your device.

The optional ElevenLabs cloud path, and what transmission involves. The App offers a single, optional cloud-based processing path that operates differently from the default. This path is engaged if, and only if, you expressly select the ElevenLabs processing engine within the App and supply your own ElevenLabs API key. It is never enabled automatically, never selected on your behalf, and never used as a silent fallback. When, and only when, you choose this engine and provide your own credentials, the specific audio you elect to process is transmitted from your device to ElevenLabs and processed through the ElevenLabs Audio Isolation API in order to isolate or clean that audio. In that circumstance the audio genuinely leaves your phone and is sent to ElevenLabs.

You should understand the following about the cloud path before you enable it:

  • You initiate it deliberately. Transmission occurs only as a result of your explicit selection of the ElevenLabs engine combined with your own API key; absent both of these, no Content is ever sent off your device by the App.
  • You use your own account and credentials. Because you supply your own ElevenLabs API key, your use of the Audio Isolation API is conducted under your own relationship with ElevenLabs. The audio you submit, and the way ElevenLabs receives, processes, retains, and deletes it, are governed by ElevenLabs' own terms of service and privacy policy, which we do not control and which apply directly to you.
  • We are not the recipient. When you use this path, the audio is transmitted to ElevenLabs for processing; it is not routed to, copied to, or stored by us. We do not maintain a server-side copy of the audio you send to ElevenLabs.
  • You should review ElevenLabs' practices. We strongly encourage you to read ElevenLabs' privacy policy and terms before enabling that engine, so that you understand how a third party will handle audio you choose to send to it, including any retention, model-training, or sub-processing practices it may apply.

We provide the ElevenLabs option as a convenience for users who prefer cloud-based isolation, and we have designed the App so that this choice is transparent and entirely within your hands.

Storage and handling of Content on your device. Output files that you create or export are written to your device storage using the storage access you grant, and they remain on your device under your control unless and until you move, share, or delete them. The App's access to the microphone, camera, and storage exists solely to enable you to record, import, process, and export Content; these permissions are described more fully elsewhere in this Policy. Temporary or intermediate files generated during local processing are handled on your device and are intended to be transient. We do not separately collect the contents of these files, and we do not transmit them as part of analytics, diagnostics, or advertising.

What is not Content, and what we may still process. The privacy-first treatment described above concerns the substance of your Content. It does not extend to the limited operational, account, analytics, diagnostic, advertising, and billing information described in other sections of this Policy. By way of illustration, the export counter retained locally through SharedPreferences records how many exports you have performed for the purpose of enforcing the Free plan's export limit and determining when a rewarded advertisement may be offered; this counter reflects the fact that an export occurred, not the content of the file you exported. Similarly, usage events collected through Firebase Analytics and crash data collected through Firebase Crashlytics may indicate that a processing operation was started, completed, or failed, but are not designed to capture, and should not contain, the audio or video itself. We instruct our analytics and diagnostics configuration to avoid the inclusion of Content, and you should not place personal or sensitive information into fields, file names, or labels that you expect to remain private.

Your responsibility for the Content you submit. You are responsible for the Content you record, import, and process, and you represent that you have the necessary rights and permissions to use it, including the consent of any individual whose voice, image, likeness, or other personal data is captured in your recordings. Where you transmit audio to ElevenLabs through the optional cloud path, you are responsible for ensuring that doing so is lawful in your circumstances, including obtaining any consents required under applicable law. You should not submit Content that you are not authorized to process or that would violate the rights of others.

Legal basis and jurisdiction. Where applicable data-protection law requires a legal basis for processing, our limited handling of Content-related information rests on the performance of the service you request and on your consent, including your explicit choice to engage the optional cloud path. Consistent with our status as a developer based in India, this processing is conducted in accordance with the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 and other applicable laws, and, where the cloud path is used, the international transfer of audio to ElevenLabs follows from your own deliberate election to use that engine under your own ElevenLabs credentials.
